## What I found
The information that I gathered regarding the existing software/hardware. existing in Cerebral Palsy are. stasism.com  This website is a physical Therapy provider for people with cerebral palsy but instead it turns physical therapy into fun video games like Mario world by having the player foot movements on a mat control the character's actions in game. I wish I learned about this sooner. Regular physical therapy is so boring. Another software I found regarding CP was gomove.org. Which is a website that allows people with CP and physical Therapist to do and provide physical Therapy from home. Where individuals can make goals catered to individual needs and track and set goals to be achieved with the help of their therapist. on to the hardware I found regarding Cerebral Palsy. One was the Exoskeleton is a wearable robot for kids with cerebral palsy to improve their lower body extremities. It tracks an individual's walking pattern, and redirects their walking pattern from a bilateral  walking gait to a straighter gait. As well as locking the joints in a brace to assist in joint bending. Another hardware I found when researching was locomat therapy. Which is also another fun way to improve a person with cerebral palsy's walking gait. Lokomat is kind of like a treadmill but it's for people with bilateral walking patterns.A huge robot is harnessed onto the person's body with arms free and then they walk in a straight line.The treadmill can be elevated to increase the difficulty and make it feel like you're climbing a hill.To make it seem not so boring a big smartboard connected to the treadmill has games that can be played while working out your walking gait.
